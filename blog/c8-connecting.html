how did it go?

When I was young man, 30 plus years ago, we called this good breeding and manners. The building of trust and sincerity came through the customs and traditions of society and acting them out through conventional socialisation processes. In those times, people didn't obsess about building trust and sincerity as they were too busy living their lives and letting such matters take their natural course. You can't even hope to build enduring trust or sincerity in an artifical manner. The stuff we see so much of in the world today is nothing more than superficial decoration that covers festering misery.

Marc Lesser states five techniques for building trust and sincerity within a group:

  1. establish trust
  2. listen (by "looping" and "dipping")
  3. asking probing and open-ended questions
  4. provide feedback
  5. partnering to create options and practices

This is where my personal life experiences come in to play, including managing diverse groups of people within workplaces. I'd say there are several features to organically growing trust and sincereity within you team, and these are:

  1. values: shared customs and traditions
  2. goals: shared existential purpose, i.e. earning a good living
  3. respect: for traditions and history
  4. courage: for taking risks and trying out new ideas
  5. likability: people in a team have to gel which means liking each other
