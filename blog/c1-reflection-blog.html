Life at EDA: Trainee Web Developer

Fireside Chat with Shereef

I've just watched a video mad by Shereef who is a Web Developer Bootcamp teacher in USA. He put it on the line as the what I might expect during and after my camp at EDA in Wellington.

what's my take on the EDA experience?

After only a few days I already have a good feel for EDA and that's warm and friendly. The EDA tutors are constant putting 100% effort into their job; teaching me and other students. Mind you they are more like guides an teachers. And that's good.

what are my impression?

It's intense from the get-go. I was initially caught in the the glare of oncoming headlights ... but ... I took a deep breath and dived into the work. How could I possibly break GitHub or a Command Line? And when I realised that I was doing heaps of this back over two decades ago on old remote UNIX machines, I thought to myself that I should stop worrying and just get on with it. To my delight I have found my fellow students and tutor very reassuring and comforting. It's a good environemnt to jump in and learn it.

How do I see myself engaging with this culture?

I'm an introvert but I love learning. I always have loved learning. I also see EDA as a place where I can explore my options without guilt, or too many restraints. Will I use my newly developed skills in the World of Data Analytics and Statistics that is oh-so familiar to me? Or will I go off and explore the front-end of the web or jump onto functional programming? Choices, choices, choices.

Have my expectations of EDA changed?

No. I just had a gut feeling that it would be goos for me. And it will be as I am determined to make it work for me. I'm running my own race. I' here tobe creative and I'm definitely not a consumer of we dev.

Am I excited to participate in this kind of learning environment?

You betcha. I have had a rather unstimulating time over the past few years. And I believe that when EDA open their doors to me, they weren't just offering me a web dev. course, but offering me a new opportunity for a better life.

How do I see myself engaging with this culture?

I'm not nervous ... I'm excited by all the possibilies which are sure to open up for me from today.
